15 BEST WATERFALLS IN ICELAND

15 Amazing Waterfalls In Iceland

Iceland is famous for its extraordinary waterfalls, each unique in size, shape, and setting. Here are 15 of the most breathtaking waterfalls to experience in Iceland:

  1. Gullfoss: One of Iceland’s most famous waterfalls, Gullfoss is a two-tiered waterfall on the Hvítá River that drops into a rugged canyon, known for its power and beauty.
  2. Seljalandsfoss: Located along the Ring Road, this 60-meter waterfall lets visitors walk behind the cascading water, offering an unusual and immersive view of the falls and the landscape.
  3. Skógafoss: A stunning, 60-meter high waterfall near Skógar, with an intense flow and a rainbow often visible on sunny days. It’s also easily accessible from the Ring Road.
  4. Dettifoss: Europe’s most powerful waterfall, located in Vatnajökull National Park, known for its raw force and mist. It’s a truly humbling sight.
  5. Goðafoss: Known as the "Waterfall of the Gods," this beautifully curved waterfall on the Skjálfandafljót River is steeped in Icelandic folklore.
  6. Kirkjufellsfoss: A small but picturesque waterfall, best known for its view of Mount Kirkjufell in the background, creating one of Iceland’s most iconic photo spots.
  7. Hraunfossar: A series of smaller waterfalls that trickle through lava fields and pour into the Hvítá River, creating a beautiful scene of water flowing over dark lava rock.
  8. Barnafoss: Just a short distance from Hraunfossar, this waterfall is famous for its striking blue waters and unique rock formations.
  9. Svartifoss: Located in Skaftafell National Park, this waterfall is framed by striking black basalt columns, creating a unique, cathedral-like backdrop.
  10. Háifoss: One of Iceland’s tallest waterfalls, Háifoss plunges 122 meters into a rugged canyon, surrounded by scenic Icelandic highland landscapes.
  11. Aldeyjarfoss: Known for its striking basalt column formations, Aldeyjarfoss is a stunning waterfall set in a dramatic, rocky landscape in the north of Iceland.
  12. Ófærufoss: This multi-tiered waterfall in the Eldgjá canyon was once famous for its natural stone bridge, which collapsed in the 1990s but still remains beautiful and remote.
  13. Dynjandi: Sometimes called “The Jewel of the Westfjords,” Dynjandi is a cascading waterfall with a wide, bridal-veil shape that flows down a mountain in multiple tiers.
  14. Glymur: Iceland’s second-tallest waterfall, with a drop of 198 meters, located in a scenic canyon. The hike to Glymur includes caves, rivers, and scenic views, making it an adventure.
  15. Fagrifoss: Translating to "Beautiful Falls," Fagrifoss is true to its name, with wide, flowing waters nestled in a remote and picturesque Icelandic landscape.
