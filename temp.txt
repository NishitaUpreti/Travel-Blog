        <p>Mount Fitz Roy, also known as Cerro Fitz Roy or Cerro Chaltén, is a stunning peak located in Patagonia, on the border between Argentina and Chile. It’s known for its jagged peaks, challenging hikes, and breathtaking views, drawing trekkers and mountaineers from around the world.</p>
        <ol>
            <li>Starting Point: El Chaltén:
                <p>The hike to Mount Fitz Roy usually begins in El Chaltén, a small town in Argentina known as the "Trekking Capital of Argentina." The town is accessible from El Calafate, a nearby city with bus services connecting the two.</p>
            </li>
            <li>Main Trails
                <ul>
                    <li>Laguna de los Tres: This is the most popular and iconic hike, providing close-up views of Fitz Roy. It's a challenging but rewarding trek with a steep final section.
                        <ul><li>Distance: ~25 km (15.5 miles) round trip</li>
                            <li>Duration: 8-10 hours</li>
                            <li>Difficulty: Moderate to difficult, with a steep incline in the last 1 km</li></ul>
                        
                    </li>
                    <li>Laguna Capri: A slightly shorter and easier option that still offers great views of Fitz Roy.
                        <ul><li>Distance: ~8 km (5 miles) round trip</li>
                            <li>Duration: 2-3 hours</li>
                            <li>Difficulty: Moderate</li>
                        </ul>
                        
                    </li>
                </ul>
                
            </li>
            <li>What to Expect
                <ul>
                    <li>Views: Fitz Roy's distinctive, sharp granite peaks are breathtaking, especially when seen from Laguna de los Tres. On clear days, you’ll have unobstructed views of the entire Fitz Roy range</li>
                    <li>Terrain: The terrain includes forests, rivers, and rocky paths. The last kilometer of the Laguna de los Tres hike is particularly steep and can be slippery.</li>
                    <li>Weather: Patagonia’s weather can be unpredictable, with strong winds and rapid temperature changes. It's best to check the weather forecast and come prepared with layers and waterproof gear.</li>
                </ul>
            </li>
            <li>Best Time to Visit
                <ul>
                    <li>Summer (December to March): This is the most popular time, with relatively stable weather and longer daylight hours.</li>
                    <li>Spring and Fall (October-November and March-April): These shoulder seasons offer fewer crowds and beautiful colors but come with a higher chance of variable weather.</li>
                </ul>
            </li>
            <li>Tips for Hikers
                <ul>
                    <li>Start Early: To enjoy a quieter experience and the best lighting for photography.</li>
                    <li>Pack Essentials: Bring plenty of water, snacks, sunscreen, a hat, and trekking poles if needed for stability.</li>
                    <li>Respect the Environment: Follow Leave No Trace principles to help preserve the pristine beauty of the area.</li>
                </ul>
            </li>
        </ol>
        <p>This hike is truly one of the best ways to experience the rugged beauty of Patagonia!</p>
